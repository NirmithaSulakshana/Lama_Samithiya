SHANTHI Cheildren's Society.

About Us

We are a sub society which is conducted under the supervison of the supervison of the person who is appointed as the head of the children's society in the annual eneral meeting of "Shanthi" community council.

History

The community board named "Shanthi" which was established in 1949 has been doing a great service in general sections. The sport section,library section , educational section, religious and welfare section, children's section arts section are the main sections of it. Among them "Shanthi" children's society was started in 1979 for the first time under the children's section. Mr Harry Lakdusinghe and Jayasinghe were the first patrons of it.

How it all began

The first chairman of the children's society was Mr Suganda Muthunayake. The first secretary was Mr Janaka Piyal Ranasinghe.

They devided the society into two houses named "Sankha" and "Padma". Not only the spiritual development of children, but also their sports, dancing, social activities were improved by

Mr Harry Lakdusinghe as the leader of the Sankha House and Mr Jayasinghe Muthunayake as the leader of the Padma House in those days. From those days this society has been doing a huge service to the village till today.

What we are doing

Shanthi children's society organizes various types of activities for recognizing hidden tallents of children and for giving a helping hard to develop children's tallens. Some of them are art competitions, speech competitions, folk song competitions, dancing competitions. Later they included kite flying competitins and volley ball tournaments.

Organizing Jamborie

Drama festivals, concers were held for improving dancing, sports skills and social activities of the children in our vllage from the begining.

Actually, this was a new path for the children to learn how to buildup effective relationships. Later the children of the society were deployed for scounting. With the aspiration of scouts beacon hights and a children's jamboree in 1986. from that day anually they hold a children's jamboree gloriously till today.
